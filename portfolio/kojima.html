The Approach & Process

The restrictions for this project were limited, and allowed for a lot of creativity and room to explore more abstract styles of design. E-commerce stores need to be engaging and clean, with a clear sense of hierarchy while remaining easy to navigate. I had researched a number of companies prior to starting the project, and the ones that caught my eye were those with large images and limited text.

Products should do a lot of the talking, so detailed product images were my main focus. Text is only there to enhance what a user can't physically feel and hold. It should inform the user about what isn't inherently obvious, like shipping, returns, weight and material.

UI Design

The Logo

My goal when approaching this design project, was to create an aesthetically pleasing and intuitive shopping experience for users. I wanted the customers to flow freely and easily between products and categories, matching the user experience with a zen-like aesthetic experience.

When deciding on a product to showcase, I combined my interests in Japanese culture and collecting kitchenware. Recently, consumers have been trending toward buying more eco-friendly and hand-made products. Natural products should accompany minimalist descriptions, since there is no need for long lists of materials or explanations. If a user was interested in the company's story, I wanted there to be a separate but well-defined history and reasoning behind the products.
